Blocks at their core essence are swaths of color (when used with background colors) used to encapsulate or demarcate a section of the page, with a bit of padding. It is reserved (generally) for sections that span the full width of the browser viewport. They used are outside of any .container, and rather the optional .container is generally nested inside. The markup is simple:

<div class="p-a bg-primary"> //.use additional class of 'text-inverse' to ensure type is white on inverse backgrounds
    <div class="container">Contents</div> //use with ".container" enforce container max/min width for responsiveness
</div>

Use with padding and margin utility spacing classes to implement various padding and margin requirements.
